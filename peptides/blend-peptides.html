Understanding the Bioavailability Barrier in Peptide Drugs

Peptides face several challenges in achieving high bioavailability. These include:

  • Enzymatic Degradation: Peptides are rapidly broken down by proteolytic enzymes in the gastrointestinal tract and bloodstream.

  • Poor Permeability: Large molecular size and hydrophilicity make it difficult for peptides to cross biological membranes.

  • Short Half-Life: Peptides are often cleared quickly from circulation, reducing their duration of action.

  • Low Stability: Peptides are susceptible to chemical degradation, leading to reduced potency.
